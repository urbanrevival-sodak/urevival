1868

The Fort Laramie Treaty establishes the Great Sioux Reservation, which includes the Pine Ridge and Rosebud reservations.

Late 1800's

The Dawes Act of 1887 aimed to assimilate Native Americans by allotting reservation lands to individual tribal members, leading to the breakup of communal landholdings.

Early 1900's

Allotment policies result in fragmented land ownership and loss of communal land.

1934

The Indian Reorganization Act (IRA) attempts to reverse allotment policies and restore tribal self-governance.

1950's-1960's

Housing conditions on reservations deteriorate; overcrowding and substandard living conditions become prevalent.

1968

The Fair Housing Act is enacted in the United States to prevent discrimination in housing.

1970's

The Indian Self-Determination and Education Assistance Act grants tribes more control over federal funding and services.

1980's

Efforts to improve housing conditions intensify; various federal programs and grants target reservation housing needs.

1990's

The Native American Housing Assistance and Self-Determination Act (NAHASDA) is passed, providing block grants to tribes for housing programs.

2000's

Tribes on Pine Ridge and Rosebud reservations work on housing initiatives, including construction, renovation, and infrastructure development.

2010's

Challenges persist, including lack of funding, infrastructure issues, and a high demand for housing on reservations.

2020's

Efforts continue to address housing disparities, with a focus on sustainable and culturally appropriate housing solutions. Initiatives for community development and economic stability become integral.
